Privacy Policy

Privacy Policy for ADHD Task Hub

Last updated April 17, 2026. ADHD Task Hub is published by Next Step Labs and designed so the core experience works on-device. This Privacy Policy explains in plain language what data the app accesses, how it is used, when it may leave your device, and how you can contact us with privacy questions.

1. Who this policy applies to

This Privacy Policy applies to ADHD Task Hub and the public website for ADHD Task Hub, both provided by Next Step Labs. ADHD Task Hub does not require an account for core use, and Next Step Labs can be reached at nextsteplabsapps@gmail.com.

2. What app data stays on your device

Tasks, lists, notes, subtasks, templates, focus sessions, reminder settings, and similar core productivity data are stored locally on your device. The app is designed to work without a mandatory login or automatic cloud sync for core task data.

3. Task photos, camera access, and photo library access

If you choose to add a task photo, ADHD Task Hub may request camera permission so you can take a picture, or photo-library access so you can select an image you choose. These permissions are optional and are used only to let you attach an image to a task.

4. How task photos are handled

When you add a task photo, the image is copied into the app's local storage area on your device so it can appear on the task. Next Step Labs does not use task photos for advertising, does not sell them, and does not automatically upload them to a Next Step Labs server.

5. Notifications and reminders

If you enable notifications, the app can schedule local reminders on your device to help you follow up on tasks you choose to track. Reminder scheduling depends on your device operating system and notification settings.

6. Backups, exports, and user-initiated sharing

ADHD Task Hub can export a local JSON backup file when you choose to do so. Importing a backup is a manual action and replaces the current local app data on the device. If you choose to export or share a backup file, that export is handled where you decide to save or send it. Task photos are stored locally on-device, and exported backups may include task records or local file references rather than a hosted copy of the image itself.

7. Analytics, diagnostics, and internet access

Some builds may include limited analytics or crash and reliability diagnostics to help improve the app. When those services are enabled, they are configured to avoid task titles, task notes, task photos, and other user-written content wherever possible. Core task management features do not require a Next Step Labs account or a backend connection.

8. How data is shared

ADHD Task Hub does not sell your personal data. Core task data is intended to remain on your device unless you choose to export, share, or back it up yourself. Limited analytics or diagnostics, when configured in a build, may be processed by service providers that help Next Step Labs understand app reliability and feature usage at an aggregate or metadata level.

9. Retention and deletion

Local app data remains on your device until you edit it, delete it, clear app storage, uninstall the app, or overwrite the data by importing a backup. If you remove a task photo or delete a task, the app attempts to remove its locally stored photo copy when it is no longer needed. Support emails or other direct messages you send to Next Step Labs may be retained for as long as reasonably needed to respond and keep a support record.

10. Changes to this Privacy Policy

If ADHD Task Hub adds new services, data flows, or privacy-relevant features later, this Privacy Policy will be updated before or alongside those changes.
